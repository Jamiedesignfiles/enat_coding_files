Enat Kitchen was opened in 2010 by an Ethiopian family wishing to bring authentic Ethiopian cuisine to Portland.

Enat |e-nat|
an Ethiopian term meaning "Mama."

A coffee ceremony is a ritualized form of making and drinking coffee. The coffee ceremony is one of the most recognizable parts Ethiopian culture. Coffee is offered
when visiting friends, during festivities, or as
a daily staple of life.Please join us in our
special ceremony. One can be provided for you upon request.

We offer a great variety of entrées featuring dishes made of beef, chicken, fish, or lamb, as well as the vegetarian’s delights such as vegetables, split peas, lentils and salad. We use herbs and spices
imported directly from Ethiopia in all of our dishes; providing you with a unique flavor. Cooked to perfection we guarantee that you will have a truly authentic experience.

At Enat, we promise that you will eat to your
heart’s content. Dining takes place in a communal setting, where you will eat out of a common plate using your hands; utensils are provided if you
wish. Ethiopian food is a fun, tasty, and healthy. So bring your friends and family for a wonderful
eating experience coupled with Ethiopian ambience and hospitality.

Of course, an Ethiopian dining experience is never complete without the taste of Ethiopian coffee where it is brewed and served fresh daily. It is a part of Ethiopian tradition that we would like to proudly share with you!
